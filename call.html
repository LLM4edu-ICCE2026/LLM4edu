Call for Workshop Papers

Share research, practice, and innovation on LLMs for education.

We invite full and short papers on LLM-based educational tools, agents, learning analytics, and human-centered applications.

Scope

A wide range of LLM-based applications is emerging, including language translation, oral training, essay correction, programming tutoring, and automatic problem-solving. Nevertheless, LLM technology has not yet fully matured, and its limitations can create barriers to grounded educational application. Students' over-reliance on these tools may also lead to negative impacts.

There remains a lack of evidence on how to properly and effectively utilize different types of assistants based on LLMs, and whether such tools can truly enhance learning. Further empirical research is urgently needed to address these issues.

Topics of Interest

  • LLM-based AI agents for education
  • Cooperative learning based on LLMs
  • Design of prompts for teaching and learning scenarios
  • Analysis of the interaction process between humans and LLMs
  • Application of LLMs in educational data analysis and decision support
  • Application of LLMs in teaching design and teaching resource development
  • Application of LLMs in personalized learning
  • Application of LLMs in automatic evaluation and feedback generation
  • Game-based learning and educational games based on LLMs
  • Human-computer interaction for LLM-integrated educational applications

Paper Format and Submission

This workshop accepts full papers (8-10 pages) and short papers (5-6 pages), including references. Accepted papers in this workshop will be included in the conference proceedings, which will be submitted to Elsevier for indexing in Scopus. Please format your paper using the official ICCE paper template.

Submit both the Word and PDF versions of your paper via email to icce2026llm4edu@gmail.com. The workshop will adopt a single-blind review process, so anonymization of the manuscript is not required.
